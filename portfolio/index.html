Education

I was interested in computers from an early age and as such I chose to do a GNVQ in ICT during high school following by an AS level in ICT. I left high school after Year 12 and went to college where I acheived a National Diploma in IT, which is equivalent to three A levels. After college I went to De Montfort university in Leicester and graduated in 2013 with an honours degree in Computer Games Programming.

Career

My first job after graduation was as a Junior Software Developer for Sertec Group Holdings Ltd based in Coleshill in the Midlands. The job involved maintaining and upgrading the in-house software which controlled all aspect of the companies operation from Financial management to staff clocking in/out systems as well as production records. After working there for a year and 4 months I felt it was time to progress my career further.

My next job working as a Process Automation Consultant for a new startup company called FSJ Consulting Ltd. During my 3 months working for FSJ I recieved foundation training in UIPath and BluePrism which taught me a lot in regards to using computers are roots to automate dull processes allowing people to do more practical work instead of the menial tasks. Unfortunately FSJ did not manage to keep the business coming in at this tiem and I was made redundant after 3 months.

I then started working for Loop Technology based in Dorchester, Dorset, as a Software Engineer. Loop Technology designs, builds, and deploys bespoke robotic systems to customers based all over the world. I have used many different technologies whilst working for Loop Technology including PLC programming, KUKA robotics, Siemens PLC logic, WPF, WCF, UWP (Universal Windows Platform), C#, Javascript, jQuery, KnockoutJS, HTML, PHP, and SQL to name a few. Other technologies and APIs are sometimes learnt on a project by project basis, depending on what the project involves.

As Loop is a small business that is growing quickly, there was an opportunity for me to redirect my career away from development and toward System Administration. I became the defacto 'IT guy' at Loop as people came to me when they had issues with their computers. From that I was tasked by the Director to implement a new network into Loop. This involved setting up a new domain controller, exchange server, data server, and active directory. Unfortunately, our existing network was running off of Windows Small Business Server 2008 which had no direct upgrade path to Windows Server 2016 which meant basically wiping the old servers and starting again.

After successfully setting up the company's new network, I was given more and more IT tasks along side my responsibilities as Software Engineer. I took on the responsibilities of taking new hardware requests, obtaining quotes from suppliers, and then setting up the new devices once ordered. I also started to manage all of the devices on our network, making sure everythign ran smoothly.

My biggest challenge to date was when I was tasked with getting Loop compliant to a level of Cyber Security known as DEFCON 658. This involved obtaining a certification called 'Cyber Security Essentials Plus' which involved an External and Internal audit. As Loop had very recently been a small company, there was not a lot in place regarding security apart from the standard anti-virus. It was a hell of a task, but I managed to get Loop certified first time. According to our assessor, Loop was only the second company, out of hundreds that he had audited, to pass first time!
